Step 1: Project overview

We compare four approaches to differentially private synthetic data generation on multi-table telemetry:

  • Wide-Table DP-VAE: Train a VAE on all columns flattened into one table.
  • Per-Table DP-SGD: Train independent VAEs per table.
  • MST: Reconstruct from noisy marginals via a Chow-Liu tree.
  • Private Evolution: LLM generates candidates; DP selects the best matches.

All methods use ε = 4.0, δ = 10−5. We benchmark on 21 SQL queries.

Step 3: Distribution comparison

This interactive chart compares real distributions against each synthetic method.

  • Select a dataset: Browser usage, RAM utilization, or display vendors.
  • Select a method: Compare any synthesis approach against the real data.
  • Toggle normalization: View as probability or raw counts.

Lower Total Variation (TV) distance means better preservation. TV < 0.15 typically passes.

Step 4: Side-by-side comparison

The panel shows a visual comparison of real vs. synthetic query results. Look for:

  • Shape match: Does the synthetic distribution follow the same shape?
  • Peak alignment: Are modes at similar locations?
  • Tail behavior: Do tails match, or is one method missing rare values?
  • Zero-inflation: Excessive zeros suggest mode collapse.

Step 5: Final scorecard

The final tally: how many queries each method passed out of its evaluated set.

Key insight: Per-table DP-SGD and MST tied at 6/21, but excel at different query types. Simple distributions pass; complex joins remain hard across all methods.

Summary

This exploration shows that differential privacy introduces a fundamental privacy-utility trade-off:

  • Simple distributions and independent features are well-preserved by most methods.
  • Join-heavy and cross-table queries remain the biggest challenge.
  • No single method dominates—a hybrid routing strategy is most promising.
